📊 Dive Into Data: Electric vs Gasoline

Use CODAP graphs and the table to investigate patterns. For each question, write a short answer and include evidence (a number, a description of a graph, or what you notice in the data).

Reminder: Your dataset includes these attributes: Manufacturer, Model, City Fuel Efficiency (MPG/MPGe), Highway Fuel Efficiency (MPG/MPGe), Primary Fuel Type, Vehicle Class, Environmental Rating (1–10).

Investigation Questions

  1. Count and Compare Fuel Types
    How many vehicles are Electric and how many are Gasoline? Which fuel type appears more often in this dataset?
    Tip: You can use the ruler icon to add counts.
  2. Compare Environmental Ratings (Median)
    Make side-by-side boxplots of Environmental Rating (1–10) for Electric vs Gasoline.
    What is the median environmental rating for Electric vehicles? What is the median for Gasoline vehicles?
    Which group has the higher median?
    Tip: Use the ruler icon to add boxplots and medians. Click "show measure labels" to see the medians as numbers.
  3. Spread / Consistency of Environmental Ratings
    Add a Normal Curve to the environmental rating data. Which fuel type has a larger spread in Environmental Rating?
    What does that tell you about how consistent the ratings are within that group?
    Tip: Larger spread means the ratings vary more from vehicle to vehicle.
  4. Compare City Fuel Efficiency
    Create a graph of City Fuel Efficiency (MPG/MPGe) split by Primary Fuel Type.
    Do Electric vehicles tend to have higher city fuel efficiency than Gasoline vehicles?
    Use the mean or median (your choice) to support your answer.
    Is there any overlap in city fuel efficiency between Electric and Gasoline vehicles?
    Tip: Overlap means some values from one group fall in the same range as the other group.
  5. City vs Highway Efficiency Relationship
    Make a scatterplot with:
    x-axis: City Fuel Efficiency (MPG/MPGe)
    y-axis: Highway Fuel Efficiency (MPG/MPGe)
    Color by: Primary Fuel Type

    What relationship do you notice between city and highway efficiency?
    Do Electric and Gasoline vehicles follow the same pattern or different patterns?
  6. Vehicle Class Patterns by Fuel Type
    Create a graph with:
    x-axis: Primary Fuel Type
    (leave the y-axis empty)
    Color by: Vehicle Class

    In Configuration, click “Fuse dots into bars” and scale by percent.
    Are Electric vehicles more likely to be in a certain vehicle class?
    Are the results expected or surprising? Explain.
    Tip: Percent bars help you compare categories fairly even if group sizes are different.
